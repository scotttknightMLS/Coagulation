Clinical & BOC Exam Notes

PT / INR Monitoring
The Prothrombin Time (PT) evaluates the extrinsic + common pathways. Patient plasma + thromboplastin + Ca²⁺ → clot. Results reported as INR for standardized warfarin monitoring. INR = (Patient PT / Mean Normal PT)ISI
Factor VII — Key BOC Facts
Factor VII has the shortest half-life (4-6 hours) of all coagulation factors. This is why the PT is the first test to become abnormal in warfarin therapy and in early vitamin K deficiency. Factor VII is Vitamin K dependent (mnemonic: 1972 = Factors II, VII, IX, X + Proteins C & S).
Crossover Activation
The TF-VIIa complex activates both Factor IX (intrinsic) and Factor X (common). This crossover is why the classic "separate pathways" model is a simplification — in vivo, the extrinsic pathway is the primary initiator of coagulation.
TFPI — Tissue Factor Pathway Inhibitor
TFPI inhibits the TF-VIIa-Xa complex, shutting down the extrinsic pathway after initial activation. This is why the intrinsic pathway amplification (via Factor IX) is necessary to sustain coagulation.
Warfarin & Factor VII
When a patient starts warfarin, Factor VII is depleted first due to its short half-life. BOC question pattern: "A patient on warfarin has an elevated PT but normal aPTT. The most likely deficient factor is: Factor VII."
